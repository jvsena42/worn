# Persona: Lucas

## Demographics

- **Age:** 18-35
- **Gender:** Male
- **Location:** Urban / suburban, mixed climate
- **Occupation:** Hybrid lifestyle — office days, social outings, casual weekends
- **Income:** Entry to mid-level — budget-conscious but willing to invest in versatile pieces

## Fashion Profile

- **Knowledge level:** Beginner — doesn't know what works together, overwhelmed by choices
- **Current wardrobe state:** Disorganized mix of impulse buys, gifts, and old favorites that don't combine well
- **Style aspiration:** Wants to look put-together without spending time or mental energy on outfits
- **Body awareness:** Unsure what fits and flatters his body type; tends to default to safe choices (black, oversized)

## Goals

1. **Look better effortlessly** — dress well daily without decision fatigue
2. **Stop buying wrong items** — understand what's missing before shopping
3. **Build versatility** — maximize outfit combinations from fewer pieces (capsule wardrobe philosophy)
4. **Gain confidence** — feel good about what he's wearing in any context (office, date, weekend)

## Pain Points

- Opens the closet and feels like he has "nothing to wear" despite owning many items
- Buys clothes impulsively and ends up with pieces that don't match anything else
- Doesn't understand fit, fabric, or color theory — picks items based on vibes alone
- Feels intimidated by fashion advice that assumes prior knowledge
- Has different contexts (work, social, casual) but tries to dress the same for all of them

## Behaviors

- Hates buying clothes — finds the whole process exhausting and overwhelming
- Shops 2-4 times per year, often seasonally or when something wears out
- Often gets home and realizes purchased pieces don't match anything else he owns
- Browses fashion content on YouTube/TikTok but rarely acts on advice
- Takes photos of items he likes in stores but forgets about them
- Keeps clothes he hasn't worn in years "just in case"

## How Worn Helps Lucas

| Pain Point | Worn Solution |
|---|---|
| "Nothing to wear" | AI shows outfit combinations from existing items |
| Impulse buying | Gap analysis tells him exactly what's missing |
| Doesn't know fit/fabric | AI auto-tags subcategory, fit, and material from photos |
| Context confusion | Profile captures lifestyle; AI tailors suggestions per occasion |
| Intimidated by fashion | Simple UI with guided chip selections, no jargon |
| Keeps unused items | Wardrobe catalog makes it visible what's actually owned |

## Key Scenarios

1. **First use:** Lucas downloads Worn, photographs his 15 most-worn items, and discovers he owns 6 black t-shirts but zero layering pieces
2. **Shopping moment:** At a store, Lucas photographs a denim jacket. "Try It" tells him it unlocks 12 new outfit combinations with his existing wardrobe
3. **Morning routine:** Lucas checks Worn's outfit suggestions for a work meeting — smart casual with pieces he already owns
4. **Seasonal review:** Gap analysis recommends a pair of chinos and white sneakers as his highest-impact next purchases

## Design Implications

- **Onboarding must be frictionless** — photo-first, AI does the heavy lifting
- **Language should be simple** — "Slim Fit" not "Tailored silhouette", "Tops" not "Upper body garments"
- **AI guidance over manual input** — auto-tag should be the default path, manual fields are fallback
- **Visual over textual** — show outfit combinations as grids, not lists
- **Celebrate progress** — show wardrobe versatility score, combinations count, items that pair well
